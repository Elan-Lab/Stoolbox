Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 4, 2025

1. Definition of Cookies

A cookie is a text file that may be saved on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. It is used to collect information about your browsing behavior in order to provide you with a personalized and secure experience.

2. Legal Framework

In accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU 2016/679) and the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC as amended), the use of cookies that are not strictly necessary for the functioning of the website requires your prior consent. The competent authority in France is the CNIL: www.cnil.fr

3. Types of Cookies Used

Our website uses the following types of cookies:

a. Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the proper functioning of the website and do not require your consent.
Examples:

  • Session cookies
  • Shopping cart cookies
  • Authentication cookies

    • b. Audience measurement cookies (analytics)

    These cookies help us understand how users interact with the website (pages visited, time spent, errors, etc.). They are only used with your explicit consent. Example: Google Analytics (with IP anonymization enabled)

    c. Functionality cookies

    These cookies remember your preferences (language, region, etc.). They can be disabled, but doing so may reduce the quality of your experience.

    d. Advertising and targeting cookies

    These cookies record your visits in order to display relevant ads on our site or on partner sites. Your explicit consent is required. Examples: Meta Pixel (Facebook), Google Ads

    4. Consent

    When you first visit the site, a cookie banner appears informing you of the purpose of the cookies used. You may:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all non-essential cookies
  • Customize your choices
    • You can modify or withdraw your consent at any time via the "Manage my cookies" link available at the bottom of each page. Consent must be free, informed, specific, and unambiguous. Simply continuing to browse does not constitute valid consent (see CNIL – Cookies and trackers).

    5. Retention Period

    Cookies are stored for a maximum of 13 months from the date they are placed on your device. Your consent is stored for a period of 6 months, after which we will ask for your consent again.

    6. Your Rights

    In accordance with the GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right to access, rectify, and erase your data
  • Right to object and withdraw your consent
  • Right to restrict processing
    • To exercise your rights, contact us at: team.elanoff@gmail.com

    7. Cookie Management Tools

    You can configure your cookie preferences through your browser settings:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge
